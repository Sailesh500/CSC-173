Image 1:

 

The graph provides a visual representation of the connections and characteristics of the entities in the data. The different colors, sizes, and link thicknesses help distinguish between entity types and highlight the varying relationships between them.

 

Nodes: The nodes are represented by circles, and their colors are determined by the colorMap object. The different colors represent different types of entities: blue for "company," green for "organization," and red for "person." The size of the nodes varies based on the nodeScale function, which maps the node degrees to different radii. Nodes with a "degree" value of "direct" have larger radii, while others have smaller radii. Links: The links between the nodes are represented by lines. The thickness of the links is determined by the linkScale function, which maps the link weights to different stroke widths. Node Labels: Each node has a tooltip-like label displayed when hovering over it. The label shows information about the node, including its ID, type, country, and degree.
